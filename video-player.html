Mitosis Chromosome
During which phase do chromosomes first become visible? My textbook says prophase but I'm not sure if that means they're actually forming or just becoming visible under a microscope. Can someone clarify?
FA
MK
Prof. Maria Khan verified Instructor 1 hour ago
Great question Fatima! Chromosomes are present in the nucleus throughout the cell cycle — during Interphase they appear as chromatin (long, thin threads). In Prophase, the chromatin condenses and coils tightly, making the chromosomes visible under a microscope. So they don't "form" in prophase — they become visible. This distinction is important for Cambridge marking schemes! 📌

Spindle Fibres Anaphase
What exactly causes the spindle fibres to shorten during anaphase? Is it an active or passive process? I need this for a 6-mark answer.
FA
MK
Prof. Maria Khan verified Instructor 4 hours ago
Excellent question for a 6-marker! Spindle fibre shortening is an active process driven by motor proteins (dynein and kinesin) that "walk" along microtubules. The kinetochore fibres depolymerise (lose tubulin subunits) at the kinetochore end, pulling the chromatids poleward. For full marks, mention: (1) motor proteins, (2) ATP hydrolysis, (3) depolymerisation of microtubules, (4) movement toward opposite poles. 📚
